BayesPI-BAR: A New Biophysical Model for Characterization of Regulatory Sequence Variations


Junbai Wang 1 and Kirill Batmanov 1

1. Department of Pathology

Oslo University Hospital - The Norwegian Radium Hospital
Montebello 0310 Oslo
Norway


Abstract


Sequence variation in regulatory DNA regions are known to cause functionally important consequences for gene expression. It is a great challenge to identify DNA sequence variations which may have essential role in determining phenotype and be linked to disease by analyzing massive genome-wide sequencing data.

Here, a new computational pipeline, Bayesian method for Protein-DNA Interaction with Binding Affinity Ranking (BayesPI-BAR), is proposed to quantify the effect of sequence variations on protein binding. BayesPI-BAR uses biophysical modeling of protein-DNA interaction to predict single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) that cause significant changes in the binding affinity of a regulatory region for transcription factors (TFs). It includes two new parameters (TF chemical potentials or protein concentrations; and direct TF binding targets) that are neglected by the previous methods.

The new method is verified at 67 known human regulatory SNPs, where 47 (~70%) have predicted true TFs ranked in the top 10. Importantly, the performance of BayesPI-BAR, which uses principal component analysis to integrate multiple predictions from various TF chemical potentials, is always better than existing programs such as sTRAP and is-rSNP, when evaluated on the same SNPs.

BayesPI-BAR is a publicly available tool and is able to perform parallelized computation, which helps to investigate a large number of TFs and SNPs and to detect disease associated regulatory sequence variations, in the sea of genome-wide non-coding regions.
